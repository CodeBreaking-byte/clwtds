    Works

    International congresses

    1. First International Congress on "Life Comes from Life" (Life Comes From Life), Vrindavana, India, November 14-16, 1977. Over 200 scientists, religious leaders, doctors and Nobel prizes participated in this four-day conference. The theory of molecular evolution has been critically examined and the origin of life has been discussed from a spiritual point of view.

    2. First World Congress for the "Synthesis between science and religion", Bombay, 1986. Over 1,100 leading scientists (including seven Nobel Prizes in science) and world religious leaders participated. His Holiness the Dalai Lama was the principal guest of the Congress and Prof. George Wald, Harvard University's Nobel Prize for Biology, opened the Congress. The prof. Joseph Weizenbaum, pioneer of artificial intelligence, MIT; Ernan McMullin, Department of Philosophy of Science, University of Notre Dame; Harvey Cox, Harvard Divinity School; John Searle, philosopher, University of California, Berkeley; Huston Smith, theologian, MIT; Paulos Mar Gregorios, president of the World Council of Churches; E. C. G. Sudarshan, Physicist at the University of Texas, Austin; and many others.

    3. First International Congress on the "Study of Consciousness in the Interior of Science", University of San Francisco, 1990. More than 400 scientists, Nobel prizes and scholars participated in this conference focused on the scientific study of consciousness in various scientific academic disciplines. Nobel prizes Sir John Eccles and Prof. George Wald opened Conresso. Other key participants were: Karl Pribram, neuropsychologist, Henry Stapp, quantum physicist; A.G. Cairns-Smith, chemist; Herbert Frohlich, biophysical; John Searle, philosopher; Benjamin Libet, neurophysiologist, etc.

    4. Second World Congress for the "Synthesis between science and religion", Calcutta, 1997. Over 2,000 eminent scientists, Nobel prizes, religious leaders, philosophers and educators from all over the world attended this conference. Prof. Charles Townes, the Nobel Prize for Physics at the University of California, Berkeley, USA, was the keynote speaker. Physicist Prof. Gregory Benford, University of California, Irvine, Dr. MM Damad, Iranian Academy of Sciences, Dr. Joe Kamiya, psychologist and pioneer of biofeedback technology, University of California, San Francisco, Dr. Glenn Bucher, President of the Graduate Theological Union, Berkeley; Prof. Jonathan Shear, Virginia Commonwealth University and Founding Editor of Journal of Consciousness Studies; Prof. Amit Goswami, quantum physicist, University of Oregon; Prof. Robert Shaw, Psychologist, University of Connecticut, etc. Dr. Robert Muller, former Assistant Secretary General of the United Nations, proposed and delivered a congress address: "The Promise of a Religious Permanent Organization". The volume of the World Congress for the "Synthesis of Science and Religion", entitled "Thoughts on the Synthesis of Science and Religion" with the preface by His Holiness the Dalai Lama was published as part of the Annual Congress of Indian Science on 3 January 2002. In this volume of 735 pages, 42 authors participated. Some of the most important contributions come from Richard Ernst, Nobel Prize in Chemistry from Switzerland with the title "Science in the third millennium: expectations between hope and fear", Deepak Chopra with the title "The Mind of God", Freeman Dyson with the title "Progress in Religion"; Jagdish Srivastava with the title: "Logic, Godel Theorem, Rationality and Spirituality"

    5. International Conference on "Science, Spirituality and the Future of Humanity", Imphal, Manipur, India, 30 April 2000. Over 400 scholars participated in this congress. The famous Indian artist, Ms. Hema Malini, spoke about the importance of spirituality in art and culture.

    6. International Conference on the "Culture of Peace", Imphal, Manipur, India, 17-19 November 2000. More than 500 international scholars participated in this conference in Manipur to reflect on the issues that humanity will have to face and, the measures for bring peace to the world. The acceptance of the existence of God and the knowledge concerning the fact that we are all sons of the one Supreme Lord will be the themes to be developed in the future to conceive world peace.

    7. Second International Congress on "Life Comes from Life" - "Life and its Origin" - "Life and its origin, scientific exploration and spiritual tradition", Rome, Italy (12-14 November 2004), at the Protomoteca hall Hall in Campidoglio and at the Auditorium Giovanni Paolo II in the Pontifical University San Tommaso D'Aquino. Over 250 scientists, Nobel prizes, religious and other thinkers from different disciplines from around the world attended the congress. The Vatican Academy with the patronage of the Pope has officially participated in this Congress by sending a speaker from the Vatican Academy and fifteen other scholars of universities connected to the Vatican: Gregoriana, Urbaniana, San Tommaso D'Aquino, etc.

    8. First International Congress "Evolutionism and Religions - Theistic Interpretation of Darwinism", 1 October 2005 at the Hall Protomoteca Hall in Campidoglio, Rome.
